Comparison with Social Media

Lets contrast this Network with the existing options for sharing knowledge on the internet :

on Facebook and Instagram , 
you would make a post and add some relevant tags , 
then you would hope that people who follow these tags , 
see your content and respond with a public comment or a private message
this is so because these are primarily platforms for social interaction and not for knowledge building 

The problem is that Facebook is not designed to help you build on top of the work of others ,
here you can directly link the note and start building upon the work , 
the platform has collaboration built in , 
and allows for non linear , non structured collaboration 

while on Facebook and Instagram you can search , and there is a lot knowledge there , 
But Facebook was not built with the primary focus of organizing knowledge in to a usable network 
which you can explore for solutions , 
On Facebook instead what you find is algorithms , 
that are optimizing for engagement , making you spend the most possible time on the platform .
This network is built only on links defined by the authors , 
we do have a basic search engine , that merely matches notes with the highest word similarity score .

But that is a simple text search algorithm , not a machine learning algorithm that is tracking your activity and predicting what content will give you the highest amount of pleasure ,so as ot make you addicted to the platform .

Here , there is no tracking , predicting , and marketing manipulation .
You see what authors like you wanted to show you .

when we consider Quora and Medium and Reddit , 
these sites are about knowledge sharing ,
and in many ways this Network is a evolution of these ideas ,

On Quora you have the question answer paradigm , 
this has been simplified here in the form of just notes , 
this changes the focus from seeking answers on questions , 
to seeking knowledge via discussion and collaboration 

On medium , there is the same concept of authors providing knowledge to readers , 
but there is no sense of converstaion , of two-way dialogue between authors and readers ,

here , through the linking feature , authors not only give out their views , 
but also create a context of the discussion , and have a collaborative discussion about the ideas shared

On Reddit , there are communities on different topics , 
and Reddit is indeed better for discovering structured knowledge ,
Things we know about and understand , things we can put into boxes and categories called subreddits ,

This network is built un-structured , un-hierarchical knowledge , 
A quote about the shortness of life , a tip about eliminating distractions , 
A perspective on a world changing invention , 
all these can be shared on Reddit ,and they already are ,

but they are all in their individual Silos , their individual subreddits , 
there is categorization , excessive categorization , 
and then category-less knowledge, which is arguably more important , 
is also forced into only category , where only perspective is used for analyzing it ,

if humans new everything there is to know , reddit would be the way to go , to go about organizing this knowledge
but since we keep discovering new things , things we don't know how to categorize , 
A link , a connection based network is better to model such knowledge ,
Reddit has no ways to build connections across multiple topics , and create a multi-dimensional addition of knowledge .
